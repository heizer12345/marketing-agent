## What is AI procurement software?

AI procurement software uses automation and machine learning to support tasks such as supplier search, intake workflows, quote analysis, document extraction, and supplier management.

Depending on the platform, it may help with:

- Supplier discovery
- Spend visibility
- Contract organization
- Approval workflows
- Quote comparison
- Supplier data management
- Intake and purchasing processes

For large organizations, these systems can be powerful because they connect procurement to finance, compliance, and internal approvals.

## What is managed sourcing?

Managed sourcing is a service-led model where a sourcing partner supports supplier discovery, verification, RFQs, communication, negotiation, and sometimes production coordination.

With a platform like **Sourcy.ai**, AI helps speed up search, matching, and workflow organization, while sourcing specialists handle the operational work that software alone usually leaves to the buyer.

## Which is better for SMEs?

For most SMEs buying physical products, **managed sourcing is often the better fit** unless the company already has an experienced procurement team.

Why? Because software does not chase suppliers, clarify incomplete quotes, or push sampling forward. Your team still has to do that.

## When AI procurement software makes sense

Software-first is usually a good fit if:

### You already have a procurement team

If you have people dedicated to supplier management, RFQs, and vendor onboarding, software can make them more efficient.

### You need internal workflow control

Enterprise-style approvals, audit trails, and process consistency are strong reasons to adopt procurement software.

### Your supplier base is already established

If your main challenge is managing existing suppliers rather than finding new manufacturers, software may be enough.

## When managed sourcing makes more sense

Managed sourcing is usually the stronger option if:

### You need new suppliers quickly

This is common for new product launches, backup suppliers, and cost-down projects.

### You are sourcing from Asia

Cross-border sourcing creates extra work around communication, lead time alignment, documentation, and supplier screening.

### You handle OEM or private label products

Custom sourcing usually involves more back-and-forth than standard catalog buying.

### Your team is small

For SMEs, the buyer is often also the product manager, founder, or operations lead. That's where managed support creates real value.

## What about cost?

Many teams assume software is always cheaper than managed sourcing. That is not always true once you factor in internal labor.

If one manager spends 10 hours per week coordinating supplier outreach, quote cleanup, and follow-up, the hidden cost is not just salary. It is also slower launches, delayed purchasing decisions, and missed margin improvements.

The better question is not “Which costs less on paper?” It is “Which gets us to the right supplier faster with less risk?”
