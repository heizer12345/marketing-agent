## Problem Section

## Why supplier verification matters before payment

Sending money to the wrong supplier can set your launch back by months. Many buyers still rely on marketplace profiles, chat replies, and PDF certificates that are hard to validate. That creates real risk—especially when you're ordering from overseas and cannot inspect every factory yourself.

Common problems buyers face:

- **Marketplace profiles can look legitimate even when key details do not match.** A supplier may show one company name online, use a different bank account for payment, and send documents tied to another entity.
- **You often do not know who is actually manufacturing your product.** A trading company may present itself like a factory, which affects price, quality control, and production visibility.
- **Document checks take time and are easy to get wrong.** Business licenses, export records, certifications, and ownership details can be difficult to review if you do not know what to compare.
- **A bad deposit decision becomes expensive fast.** Losing a 30% upfront payment on a $20,000 order means $6,000 at risk before production even starts.

There is a better way than browsing listings and hoping for the best.

To verify suppliers before payment, check whether the business name, address, bank account, documents, and manufacturing claims match across sources. ## What is supplier due diligence?
Supplier due diligence is the process of reviewing a supplier's legitimacy, capability, and risk before approving them for business. It goes beyond a simple profile check and can include company details, documentation, ownership signals, production fit, and payment-related verification.
