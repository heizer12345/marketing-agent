# Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)

## When to use
- Short copy where you need to drive action fast (ads, email subject + opener, hero sections).
- The reader already feels the problem. You're naming it back to them — not introducing it.
- Avoid for unaware audiences (they don't believe the problem yet).

## Structure
1. **Problem** — name the specific pain in the reader's words.
2. **Agitate** — show the cost of leaving it unfixed (lost time, lost money, ongoing frustration).
3. **Solve** — present the product as the relief, with one specific proof point.

## Rules of thumb
- Never invent a problem. The agitation only works when it's real.
- Don't agitate longer than you solve. Roughly 1:1 ratio of "twist" to "relief".
- Solve with one specific number or proof point, not a list of features.

## Examples

**Bad (generic):**
> "Sourcing from Asia is hard. We make it easy."

**Good (PAS, Sourcy):**
> Most importers waste their first month on Alibaba — 50 RFQs, 30 broken-English replies, one supplier ghosting mid-deal.
> A wasted month is a missed launch window, blown ad budget burning to a 404 page, and a sourcing list still empty.
> Sourcy quotes your sourcing list in 3 hours. Verified suppliers, real factories, one managed contact.

## Template prompt
"Write a {channel} using Problem-Agitate-Solve. Problem: {specific_pain}. Agitate by showing {ongoing_cost}. Solve with {sourcy_proof_point}. Keep it under {word_count} words. Match Sourcy voice."
