# Eugene Schwartz — 5 Awareness Stages

Match the copy to where the reader actually is. Mismatched copy is the #1 reason great copy still fails.

## The 5 stages
1. **Unaware** — doesn't know they have a problem. Hook with a relatable observation, then introduce the pain.
2. **Problem-Aware** — feels the pain, doesn't know solutions exist. Hook with the pain, then frame the category.
3. **Solution-Aware** — knows solutions exist, doesn't know your product. Hook with comparison vs alternatives.
4. **Product-Aware** — knows your product, hasn't bought. Hook with proof, objection-handling, risk reducer.
5. **Most-Aware** — already wants it. Hook with offer, deadline, or specific deal.

## Where each shows up
- Cold ads to broad audiences → usually Unaware or Problem-Aware.
- Retargeting ads → Solution-Aware or Product-Aware.
- Email to existing list → often Solution-Aware or Product-Aware.
- "/pricing" landing page → Most-Aware.

## Sourcy examples by stage

**Unaware**: "Most product teams don't realize 60% of their launch delays are sourcing-related."
**Problem-Aware**: "Tired of Alibaba RFQ ping-pong eating your launch window?"
**Solution-Aware**: "Sourcy vs Alibaba: managed sourcing in 3 hours, not 3 weeks."
**Product-Aware**: "Why 390 brands switched to Sourcy — and what their first quote looked like."
**Most-Aware**: "Get your first sourcing quote free. No commitment."

## Rules of thumb
- One stage per asset. Mixing stages dilutes both.
- Most marketing assumes Solution-Aware by default. Cold audiences are almost never there.
- If unsure: pre-test with two creatives at adjacent stages, measure CTR difference.
