# Social Proof Ladder

Not all proof is equal. The higher the rung, the more conversion lift it earns.

## The ladder (lowest to highest)
1. **Self-claim** — "We're the best." (Essentially zero weight.)
2. **Expert claim** — "Industry experts say..." (Weak unless named.)
3. **User count** — "390+ customers". (Solid floor.)
4. **Customer logos** — recognizable brands. (Strong.)
5. **Named testimonial with photo + title** — "Sara K., Head of Sourcing at X." (Stronger.)
6. **Quantified customer outcome** — "Cut sourcing time from 21 days to 3 hours." (Very strong.)
7. **Independent press / case study from a credible outlet** — TechCrunch, Forbes, named industry pubs. (Strongest.)

## Rules of thumb
- One high-rung proof point beats 10 low-rung ones.
- Combine rungs intentionally: a user-count near the hero, named outcomes near features, logos near pricing.
- Never use stock photos for testimonials — readers spot it instantly.
- If you don't have rung 5+ yet, double down on rung 6 (quantified outcomes) and rung 4 (logos).
