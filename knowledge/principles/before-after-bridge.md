# Before–After–Bridge (BAB)

## When to use
- Outcome-led ads.
- Case studies (this is the natural case-study structure).
- Short emotional copy where the reader needs to feel the contrast.

## Structure
1. **Before** — the world without the solution. Specific, painful, recognizable.
2. **After** — the world with the solution. Specific, calm, concrete.
3. **Bridge** — your product is what gets them from Before to After.

## Rules of thumb
- Specific beats general. "Three weeks waiting for an Alibaba reply" beats "long supplier searches".
- The After must contain a real outcome (numbers, time saved, money saved, less stress).
- The Bridge should be one or two sentences — it's the lightest part.

## Sourcy example

**Before**: "Spreadsheet of 50 Alibaba RFQs. 3 weeks of broken-English replies. Two suppliers ghost on samples. Your launch window is gone."

**After**: "One chat with Sourcy. First real quote in 3 hours. Samples shipped in 7 days. Production starts Monday."

**Bridge**: "Sourcy's AI matches your spec to verified factories instantly. A sourcing manager closes the loop on quotes and QA the same day."

## Template prompt
"Write a BAB-structured {channel}. Before: {specific_pain}. After: {specific_outcome with numbers}. Bridge: {sourcy_mechanism in 2 sentences max}. Match Sourcy voice."
