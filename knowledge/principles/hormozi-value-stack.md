# Hormozi Value Stack

## The formula
> Perceived Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

To increase perceived value: raise the top, shrink the bottom.

## How to use it on a page
1. **Dream Outcome** — name what the reader actually wants in their own words. Not the feature; the after-state.
2. **Perceived Likelihood** — stack proof: number of customers, named brands, success metrics, guarantees.
3. **Time Delay** — collapse the gap between buying and result. ("Quote in 3 hours" not "fast turnaround".)
4. **Effort & Sacrifice** — strip every step the reader has to do. ("Drop a spec. We do the rest.")

## Rules of thumb
- A guarantee is the single highest-leverage move on perceived likelihood.
- Time delay is usually the easiest lever to win on — name a literal time.
- Don't list 12 features. Stack 3 of these four dimensions hard.

## Example for Sourcy
**Dream outcome**: launch your next product with a reliable supplier, on time.
**Perceived likelihood**: 390+ brands, $33.4M sourced, verified factories, end-to-end QA.
**Time delay**: first real quote in 3 hours. Samples in 7 days.
**Effort**: one chat, one contact, no Alibaba RFQ ping-pong.

## Template prompt
"Build a value stack section for {product}. Dream outcome (in customer language): {outcome}. Perceived likelihood — stack: {proof_points}. Time delay — collapse to: {time}. Effort — minimize to: {steps}. Output as a single sharp section, no fluff."
