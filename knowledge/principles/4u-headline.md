# 4U Headline Rule — Useful, Urgent, Unique, Ultra-Specific

A headline must be all four. Most marketing headlines fail at least one. Score each on 1–4 and re-write anything under 3 across the board.

## The four U's
- **Useful** — promises a concrete benefit the reader actually wants.
- **Urgent** — gives a reason to read now, not later.
- **Unique** — sounds like nobody else's headline.
- **Ultra-Specific** — uses a number, name, or sharp detail. Vague headlines lose.

## Scoring
For each U, score 1 (fails) to 4 (nails it). A passable headline scores 12+ out of 16. A great one scores 14+.

## Examples (with scores)

**"Source Smarter with Sourcy"**
Useful 2 (vague benefit), Urgent 1, Unique 1 (could be any platform), Specific 1. Total 5. Rewrite.

**"Get Your First Supplier Quote in 3 Hours — Free"**
Useful 4, Urgent 3 (implied), Unique 3 (specific time anchor), Specific 4. Total 14. Ship it.

**"How 390 Brands Cut Their Sourcing Time by 80% Using AI Suppliers"**
Useful 4, Urgent 2, Unique 4, Specific 4. Total 14. Strong for blog/LP hero.

## Rules of thumb
- Specific numbers beat round numbers. "3 hours" > "fast". "390 brands" > "hundreds".
- Curiosity creates urgency if you can't manufacture a deadline.
- Unique often = a specific frame the competitor can't claim.

## Template prompt
"Generate 5 headline candidates for {asset_type} about {topic}. Each must be Useful, Urgent, Unique, Ultra-Specific. Score each 1–4 on all four U's. Only present headlines scoring 12+/16. Match Sourcy voice."
