# AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

## When to use
- Longer copy: landing pages, sales pages, long-form ads, cold emails.
- Cold or solution-aware audiences who need to be walked through the funnel inside the copy itself.
- When you have room to build narrative — not for snappy short headlines.

## Structure
1. **Attention** — interrupt the scroll with a hook (specific number, contrarian claim, named pain).
2. **Interest** — expand the hook into context the reader recognizes as their world.
3. **Desire** — paint the after-state with concrete proof (numbers, customer quotes, mechanisms).
4. **Action** — one CTA, low friction, with a risk reducer.

## Rules of thumb
- Each step earns the next. If Attention fails, Desire never gets read.
- Desire is where most copy is weakest — pile on specific proof, not adjectives.
- One Action per page. Don't split attention.

## Examples

**Attention**: "Your last sourcing list took 3 weeks. Your next one will take 3 hours."
**Interest**: "Most brand teams send out RFQs and wait. By the time half the suppliers reply, your product roadmap has moved on. Sourcy's AI matches your spec to 5–8 verified factories in minutes and a sourcing manager closes the loop on quotes the same day."
**Desire**: "390+ brands have sourced through us. Average first-quote: under 3 hours. We handle QA, customs, and DDP shipping — the parts that usually eat 60% of your time."
**Action**: "Drop in a spec. Get your first quote free. No commitment."
