# Geographic Market Knowledge Base for Sourcy Global

## Indonesia (ID)

### Digital Landscape
- Internet users: ~215M (77% penetration), growing 3-4% YoY
- Mobile-first market: 96% of internet access via mobile; desktop is secondary
- Dominant search engine: Google (97%+ market share)
- Key platforms: WhatsApp (business communication standard), Tokopedia, LinkedIn for B2B
- Average page load tolerance: low; optimize for 3G/4G connections outside Jakarta

### B2B Sourcing Characteristics
- Bahasa Indonesia content is essential; English-only pages lose 60-70% of potential traffic
- Key sourcing sectors: textiles/garments, palm oil derivatives, rubber, footwear, furniture, nickel
- Business culture: relationship-driven; trust signals (government partnerships, certifications) matter heavily
- WhatsApp CTA outperforms email CTA by 3-5x for lead generation
- Payment preferences: bank transfer (BCA, Mandiri dominant), limited credit card adoption in B2B
- Decision cycles involve multiple stakeholders; expect 2-4 week qualification period

### SEO/Content Notes
- Target ".co.id" ccTLD or subdomain/subfolder with hreflang id-id
- Local keywords differ from direct translation; use colloquial Indonesian search terms
- Popular B2B search patterns: "[product] supplier Jakarta", "pabrik [product]" (factory), "distributor [product]"

## Philippines (PH)

### Digital Landscape
- Internet users: ~85M (73% penetration)
- Highest social media usage globally (~4 hrs/day average)
- English proficiency is high; bilingual content (English + Filipino) works well
- Mobile-dominant: 92% mobile internet access
- Facebook is the de facto internet for many users; significant B2B discovery happens on FB groups

### B2B Sourcing Characteristics
- Key sourcing sectors: electronics assembly, BPO/services, coconut products, seafood, semiconductors
- English content performs well (official business language), but Taglish (Tagalog-English mix) resonates in ads
- Strong US trade ties; many Filipino suppliers already export to US
- Business communication: email works better here than most SEA markets; Viber also popular
- Price sensitivity is high; emphasize cost savings and free platform features

### SEO/Content Notes
- English-language SEO captures most B2B search volume
- Target both "Philippines" and "Filipino" in keyword research
- Common patterns: "[product] manufacturer Philippines", "[product] exporter Cebu/Manila"

## Thailand (TH)

### Digital Landscape
- Internet users: ~61M (85% penetration, highest in SEA)
- Mobile penetration: 93%; LINE is the dominant messaging app (not WhatsApp)
- Thai language content is mandatory for local search; English captures only export-oriented queries
- Google dominates search; YouTube is second largest search engine

### B2B Sourcing Characteristics
- Key sourcing sectors: automotive parts, electronics, rubber, food processing, jewelry, plastics
- Thai business culture: hierarchical, formal initial contact expected
- LINE Official Account is the preferred business communication channel
- Thailand Board of Investment (BOI) incentives make it attractive for manufacturing
- Decision-making is slower; respect for seniority means senior stakeholder buy-in is essential

### SEO/Content Notes
- Thai-language content is critical; direct translation loses nuance
- Target: "โรงงาน [product]" (factory), "ผู้ผลิต [product]" (manufacturer), "ซัพพลายเออร์ [product]" (supplier)
- Hreflang: th-th; ensure proper Thai character rendering

## Brazil (BR)

### Digital Landscape
- Internet users: ~187M (87% penetration), largest market in LatAm
- Mobile-first: 91% mobile access; but desktop still significant for B2B research (35-40% of B2B traffic)
- Portuguese (not Spanish) is mandatory; English content gets minimal traction
- WhatsApp is universal for business communication (98% of businesses use it)
- Google dominates search (95%+)

### B2B Sourcing Characteristics
- Key sourcing sectors: agriculture (soy, coffee, beef), mining, automotive, textiles, aerospace parts
- "Nota Fiscal" (tax invoice system) creates complexity; sourcing platform must understand local tax/trade
- Business culture: personal relationships before business transactions; video calls and in-person meetings valued
- Brazil's import regulations are complex; content explaining import processes adds high value
- BNDES (national development bank) financing is common for large purchases

### SEO/Content Notes
- Brazilian Portuguese, not European Portuguese (different vocabulary, spelling)
- Target: "fornecedor de [product]" (supplier), "fabricante de [product]" (manufacturer)
- ".com.br" domain or /br/ subfolder with hreflang pt-br
- Mercado Livre (MercadoLibre) is a competitor for product discovery

## Mexico (MX)

### Digital Landscape
- Internet users: ~100M (78% penetration)
- Mobile-first: 89% mobile access
- WhatsApp dominant for business communication
- Google: 94% search market share
- Spanish-language content required; US-Spanish and Mexican-Spanish differ in B2B terminology

### B2B Sourcing Characteristics
- Key sourcing sectors: automotive (largest auto parts exporter to US), aerospace, electronics, medical devices, agriculture
- Nearshoring boom: Mexico benefits massively from US-China decoupling; highlight this in content
- USMCA (trade agreement) is a major selling point for US buyers sourcing from Mexico
- Maquiladora / IMMEX program knowledge is valuable content for US buyers
- Business culture: relationship-oriented, meetings run long, trust before transaction

### SEO/Content Notes
- Mexican Spanish localization; avoid Spain-Spanish or Argentine-Spanish terms
- Target: "proveedor de [product] Mexico", "fabricante [product] Monterrey/Guadalajara"
- Hreflang: es-mx
- Nearshoring content has massive search volume growth (200%+ YoY for related terms)

## United States (US)

### Digital Landscape
- Internet users: ~310M (92% penetration)
- Desktop still significant for B2B: 55-60% of B2B research happens on desktop
- Google: 88% search share; Bing at 7% (worth targeting for B2B, higher B2B user share)
- LinkedIn is the primary B2B discovery and networking platform
- Email remains the standard B2B communication channel

### B2B Sourcing Characteristics
- Key sourcing needs: manufacturing alternatives to China, nearshoring to Mexico, cost-competitive SEA suppliers
- Compliance-heavy: FDA, EPA, CPSC requirements; content addressing compliance wins trust
- Procurement teams use RFP/RFQ processes; platform must support structured quoting
- Longer sales cycles for enterprise (3-6 months); faster for SMB importers (2-4 weeks)
- Content should emphasize supply chain diversification, risk mitigation, total cost of ownership

### SEO/Content Notes
- Highest competition for English sourcing keywords; focus on long-tail and country-specific combinations
- Target: "manufacturers in [country]", "[product] sourcing alternatives to China", "nearshoring [product]"
- US buyers search in English but may need content about non-English speaking supplier countries
- Comparison content performs well: "Vietnam vs Indonesia for [product] manufacturing"
